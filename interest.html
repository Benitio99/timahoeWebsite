Points of Interest

There are a number of interesting landmarks in Timahoe and the surrounding areas that are definitely worth a visit...

Timahoe Round Tower

Timahoe Round Tower
Height29m
Doorway TypeRomanesque
Window Type5 angle-headed (4 in bell storey), 2 lintelled
Number of Windows7
Ground to Doorway5 Metres
Distinguishing FeaturesExtensive & intricate doorway carving
Traditional AssociationSt. Mochua

Location: The round tower stands in a lovely setting across a picturesque footbridge that crosses the Bauteogue river in the center of the village.

Features: The Round Tower is completely intact, escept for the floors or ladders. It has one a great example of a Romanesque doorway, with extensive & intricate doorway carving with interlace, capitals and chevrons. It is unique in round tower architecture in Ireland. Romanesque doorways, usually reserved for more important buildings, are a labour-intensive decoration on a flat surface making the fact it was constructed on a curved surface and off the ground all the more impressive.

History: Saint Mochua, who died in 657, established a monastery here in the seventh-century. Burned down in 1142, it was re-founded by the O'Mores, one of the seven septs of Laois. The twelfth-century Round Tower is all that remains of the original monastery. After the ‘Suppression’ in 1556, the monastery and lands were granted to Sir Thomas Loftus and later (1609) to Richard Cosby. It was probably one of the Cosby family who transformed what was a medieval church into a castle. The last friar of the monastery was killed in 1650.

On the same setting as the tower stands a ruin, once a 15th century church (with blocked archway) that had been fortified as a "castle" in the 17th century. Only the east wall of the castle, incorporating the arch of the medieval church remains. Close by is also a heritage centre where more information can be found about the local area. This building was the local library up until recently and before that, a protestant church.

Rock of Dunamaise


Just 10 minutes drive from Timahoe is the prominent features of the Rock of Dunamaise. This Fortress stands atop a 45-metre mound overlooking the valley of the O' Mores

This Fortress has seen much upheaval over the centuries with various forces fighting for control of this strategic monument to history. Please visit and venture back in time.
